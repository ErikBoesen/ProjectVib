Smartphones are powerful tools. With the device in our pocket, we can communicate with millions of people, take photos, and access nearly all the information known to man. But since the advent of these devices, people aren't getting closer. If anything, we're getting further apart.

The average person spends four hours of their life using their smartphones, time that they could be spending with people, or doing more useful things.

Take the #VibPledge!

Now that you've made your pledge, here's how you're going to quit overusing your smartphone.

The 12-Step Program

  1. Don't keep your phone with you when you sleep. Ever. If you're a minor or live with your parents, ask them to keep it overnight, and charge it in their room. If not, shut your phone all the way down when you get in bed.
  2. Delete any app you don't really need. This includes those you use a lot, but can stop using without negative effects. For example, if you spend large amounts of time browsing Instagram by reflex, try deleting it and seeing how you do. Delete all games.
  3. Try to use your phone for two days on a single charge (or only partially charge your phone every day). This will force you to use your phone less to conserve battery, and will help you realize what is most important on your phone.
  4. Take a break from a specific app for a specific period of time and dedicate that free time to learning something new, like snowboarding or introducing yourself to a new language.
